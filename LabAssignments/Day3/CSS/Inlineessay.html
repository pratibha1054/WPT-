Essay on Army

Indian Army Day, celebrated annually on January 15th, honors the Indian Army’s valour, sacrifices, and the historic transfer of power from British to Indian command in 1949. Marked by parades, gallantry awards, and weapon displays, the day commemorates Field Marshal K.M. Cariappa’s takeover, highlighting the nation's military sovereignty and commitment to "Service Before Self"

Army Day marks the day in 1949 when General (later Field Marshal) K.M. Cariappa took over as the first Indian Commander-in-Chief from British General Sir Francis Butcher. This transition solidified India's military autonomy following independence, occurring amidst the challenging post-partition era. It is a day to pay tribute to the soldiers who sacrificed their lives to protect the nation's borders and ensure internal security.

Army Day is not just a celebration but a reminder of the priceless gift of safety provided by our soldiers. It is a day for every citizen to express gratitude, pay respect to the veterans, and inspire the youth to respect the sacrifices that maintain the country’s freedom and unity.
